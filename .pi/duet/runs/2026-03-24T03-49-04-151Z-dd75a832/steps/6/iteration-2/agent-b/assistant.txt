I found two material defects and fixed them:

1. Wrong-plan mutation bug on post-lock remove/edit  
   - `POST /api/plans/{id}/scope-changes` ignored the route `planId` for REMOVE and EDIT.
   - A commit from another plan could be changed through the wrong plan route.
   - Fixed by passing `planId` into `ScopeChangeService.removePostLockCommit(...)` and `editPostLockCommit(...)`, and validating the commit belongs to that plan.

2. Missing action-specific request validation for scope changes  
   - `ADD` with missing `commitData`, `REMOVE` with missing `commitId`, or `EDIT` with missing `commitId`/`changes` could fall through to null-driven failures instead of returning a proper 400.
   - Fixed by validating the request shape in `ReconcileController` and adding service guards for null `commitData` / `changes`.
