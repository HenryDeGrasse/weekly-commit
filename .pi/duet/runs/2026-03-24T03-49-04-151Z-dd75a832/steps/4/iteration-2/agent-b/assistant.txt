I found two material defects and fixed them:

1. Commit ownership on create was wrong  
   - `CommitService.createCommit(...)` was assigning `ownerUserId` from the request actor header (or a zero UUID), instead of the weekly plan owner.
   - Fixed to always set the commit owner to `plan.getOwnerUserId()`.

2. Update/delete endpoints did not enforce that the commit belonged to the `{planId}` in the route  
   - `PUT /api/plans/{id}/commits/{commitId}` and `DELETE /api/plans/{id}/commits/{commitId}` ignored the path `planId`, so a commit from another plan could be modified/deleted through the wrong plan route.
   - Fixed by passing `planId` into the service methods and validating the commit belongs to that plan.
